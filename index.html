About Me

✏ Personal bio I am a postdoc researcher working under Dr. Stephen Antonoplis at the University of California, Riverside. My research aims to understand how people navigate school and the society, who achieve higher status in these systems, and what their experiences are in attaining these status. More specifically, who may achieve upward mobility and what are the costs? My research bridges theoretical perspectives and methodologies from personality, social, and developmental psychology. I focus on adolscents and young adults (especially individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds) with regard to their educational outcomes, and mid-aged and older adults with regard to their class identity. I am particularly interested in how personality and contextual factors interactively shape the process and consequences of achiving higher status in these systems.
