About Me

I am a postdoctoral scholar working in the Self & Society Lab under Dr. Stephen Antonoplis at the University of California, Riverside.

My research aims to understand how people navigate school and society, including who achieves higher status in these systems and how people make sense of their experiences in attaining status. For example, I have examined who achieves educational success [see this paper] and what the costs of doing so are for individuals [see this paper], and I am particularly interested in how personality and contextual factors interactively shape the process and consequences of achieving higher status [see this paper].

My research bridges theoretical perspectives and methodologies from personality, social, and developmental psychology, including emphases on how adolescents and young adults (especially from disadvantaged backgrounds) navigate educational institutions and on how middle-aged and older adults form a class identity. I use a mixture of longitudinal panel datasets, archival government data, and experience sampling studies to address my research questions.
